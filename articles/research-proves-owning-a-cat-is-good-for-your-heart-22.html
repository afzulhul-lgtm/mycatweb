Research Proves Owning a Cat Is Good for Your Heart

By Dr. Amelia Richardson DVM, Senior Veterinary Editor
Published: 06 Mar, 2026 Updated: 06 Mar, 2026 6 Min Read

As veterinarians, we've long known that the bond between humans and animals is a powerful one, with numerous benefits for both parties. Recent research has shed light on just how profound this connection can be, particularly when it comes to the relationship between cat ownership and heart health. At our practice, we've seen firsthand the positive impact that cats can have on their owners' lives, from reducing stress and anxiety to promoting emotional wellbeing and even helping to prevent heart disease.

Introduction to the Benefits of Cat Ownership

Owning a cat can have a significant impact on both mental and physical health, with benefits ranging from reduced stress and anxiety to lower blood pressure and a decreased risk of heart disease. But what is it about cats that makes them so good for our hearts? One key factor is the emotional support they provide, which can be especially important for individuals who live alone or have limited social interaction. Cats offer companionship, affection, and a sense of purpose, all of which can help to reduce feelings of loneliness and isolation.

Studies have shown that simply petting a cat can lower blood pressure, heart rate, and stress levels, while also increasing feelings of relaxation and calmness. This is due in part to the release of oxytocin, often referred to as the "love hormone," which is stimulated by social bonding activities like petting or cuddling with a cat. Additionally, the sound of a cat's purr has been shown to have a healing effect on the body, with frequencies between 20-140 Hz having been found to be therapeutic for bone growth, pain relief, and even heart health.

As veterinarians, we recommend that cat owners take advantage of the many benefits of cat ownership by spending quality time with their pets, including activities like petting, cuddling, and simply sitting together in a quiet space.

The Benefits of Cat Purring

The purring of a cat is a unique phenomenon that has been found to have a range of benefits for both cats and humans. Not only does it serve as a form of communication and self-soothing for cats, but it has also been shown to have a healing effect on the body. The vibrations from a cat's purr have been found to be therapeutic for a range of conditions, including bone growth, pain relief, and even heart health.

Benefit Description
Reduced Stress and Anxiety Petting a cat has been shown to lower stress and anxiety levels, promoting relaxation and calmness.
Lower Blood Pressure Studies have found that cat owners tend to have lower blood pressure than non-cat owners, reducing the risk of heart disease.
Improved Mental Health The emotional support provided by cats can help to reduce symptoms of depression and anxiety, promoting overall mental wellbeing.

Expert Tips for Cat Owners

As veterinarians, we recommend that cat owners take a range of steps to promote the health and wellbeing of both themselves and their pets. This includes providing a nutritious diet, ensuring regular veterinary care, and engaging in activities that promote social bonding and emotional support. By following these tips, cat owners can help to maximize the benefits of cat ownership while also promoting the health and happiness of their pets.

Creating a Healthy Environment

Creating a healthy environment for your cat is essential for promoting their physical and mental wellbeing. This includes providing a nutritious diet, ensuring access to fresh water and litter, and creating a safe and comfortable living space. By taking these steps, cat owners can help to promote the health and happiness of their pets, while also reducing the risk of illness and disease.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

While owning a cat can have a range of benefits for both physical and mental health, there are also a number of common mistakes that cat owners should avoid. This includes failing to provide regular veterinary care, not creating a safe and comfortable living environment, and not engaging in activities that promote social bonding and emotional support. By avoiding these mistakes, cat owners can help to promote the health and wellbeing of their pets, while also maximizing the benefits of cat ownership.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the benefits of owning a cat are clear, with research showing that cat ownership can have a significant impact on both mental and physical health. By providing emotional support, reducing stress and anxiety, and promoting social bonding, cats can play a vital role in promoting overall wellbeing. As veterinarians, we recommend that cat owners take advantage of the many benefits of cat ownership by spending quality time with their pets, providing a healthy environment, and engaging in activities that promote social bonding and emotional support. By following these tips, cat owners can help to maximize the benefits of cat ownership while also promoting the health and happiness of their pets.

Disclaimer: While the benefits of cat ownership are clear, it's essential to remember that cats are living creatures that require proper care and attention. If you're considering bringing a cat into your home, be sure to do your research and consult with a veterinarian to ensure that you're prepared to provide the necessary care and attention.
