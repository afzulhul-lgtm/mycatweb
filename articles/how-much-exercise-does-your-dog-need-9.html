How Much Exercise Does Your Dog Need?

By Dr. Amelia Richardson DVM, Senior Veterinary Editor
Published: 02 Mar, 2026 Updated: 02 Mar, 2026 5 Min Read

As veterinarians, we understand the importance of regular exercise for our canine companions. Exercise is crucial for maintaining your dog's physical and mental health, and it's essential to provide them with the right amount of activity to prevent boredom, obesity, and other health problems. In this article, we will explore the exercise needs of dogs, including how much to walk your dog, daily exercise requirements, and breed-specific exercise needs.

Table of Contents

  • Understanding Dog Exercise Needs
  • Breed Exercise Requirements
  • Expert Tips for Dog Exercise
  • Common Mistakes to Avoid
  • Conclusion

Understanding Dog Exercise Needs

Dogs require regular exercise to stay happy and healthy. The amount of exercise your dog needs depends on their age, breed, size, and individual energy level. Puppies, for example, require more frequent and shorter periods of exercise, while adult dogs need more extended periods of activity. Senior dogs, on the other hand, may require less strenuous exercise due to potential health issues.

Regular exercise can help prevent a range of health problems, including obesity, diabetes, and heart disease. It can also help reduce stress and anxiety, improve mental health, and strengthen the bond between you and your dog.

Breed Exercise Requirements

Different breeds have unique exercise needs, and it's essential to understand these requirements to provide your dog with the right amount of activity. For example, high-energy breeds like Border Collies and Australian Shepherds require at least 1-2 hours of exercise per day, while low-energy breeds like Bulldogs and Pugs require much less.

Breed Exercise Needs
Border Collie 1-2 hours per day
Australian Shepherd 1-2 hours per day
Bulldog 30 minutes per day
Pug 30 minutes per day

It's also important to consider your dog's individual energy level, regardless of breed. Some dogs may require more or less exercise than their breed suggests, and it's crucial to tailor their exercise routine to their unique needs.

Daily Exercise for Dogs

The daily exercise needs of dogs vary depending on their age, breed, and size. Generally, dogs require at least 30 minutes of exercise per day, but this can range from 1-2 hours or more for high-energy breeds. It's essential to provide a mix of physical and mental stimulation to keep your dog happy and healthy.

As a veterinarian, I always recommend a minimum of 30 minutes of exercise per day for dogs, but this can vary depending on the individual dog's needs. It's also important to provide a mix of physical and mental stimulation to prevent boredom and stress.

Expert Tips for Dog Exercise

Here are some expert tips for providing your dog with the right amount of exercise:

  • Start with short periods of exercise and gradually increase the duration and intensity as your dog becomes more comfortable.
  • Provide a mix of physical and mental stimulation, including walks, runs, playtime, and training sessions.
  • Consider your dog's individual energy level and tailor their exercise routine accordingly.
  • Make sure to provide regular breaks and rest periods to prevent exhaustion and injury.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Here are some common mistakes to avoid when it comes to dog exercise:

  • Over-exercising your dog, especially in hot weather or for puppies and senior dogs.
  • Under-exercising your dog, leading to boredom, obesity, and other health problems.
  • Not providing a mix of physical and mental stimulation, leading to boredom and stress.
  • Not considering your dog's individual energy level and tailoring their exercise routine accordingly.

Conclusion

In conclusion, providing your dog with the right amount of exercise is crucial for their physical and mental health. By understanding your dog's individual exercise needs, considering their breed and energy level, and providing a mix of physical and mental stimulation, you can help keep your furry friend happy and healthy. Remember to always consult with a veterinarian if you have any concerns about your dog's exercise needs or health.

Please note that the information provided in this article is for general guidance only and should not be considered as a substitute for professional veterinary advice. If you have any concerns about your dog's health or exercise needs, please consult with a veterinarian.
